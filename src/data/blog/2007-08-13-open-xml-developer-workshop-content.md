Over the last few months, [Doug](http://blogs.msdn.com/dmahugh/), [Wouter](http://blogs.infosupport.com/wouterv/), [Brian](http://blogs.msdn.com/brian_jones/) and lots of other people delivered a series of OpenXML workshops around the globe. Those who were there, and those who weren't, can now [download all content of these workshops from OpenXMLdeveloper.org](http://openxmldeveloper.org/articles/DeveloperWorkshopContent.aspx).

There are some [interesting slideshows](http://openxmldeveloper.org/files/1922/download.aspx) available for those of you who never heared about OpenXML before, as well as some nice code samples to get you started developing in [C#](http://openxmldeveloper.org/files/1934/download.aspx) or [Java](http://openxmldeveloper.org/files/1935/download.aspx).

As a side note: I also spotted [a good article on OpenXML](http://msdn.microsoft.com/msdnmag/issues/07/08/OPC/), explaining the Open Packaging Conventions with a self-created file format (based on OpenXML).
